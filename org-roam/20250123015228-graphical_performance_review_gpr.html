1. The problem

The performance review is the process of evaluating people's contribution to a group's shared goal. Typically, it happens in companies where a team needs to evaluate employees' work, which is used to reward them with monetary bonuses, to promote them to another position, or to demand performance inprovements.

There is an extensive pain points list related to them, but I will reference just a few.

\Citet{fournier2017manager} points how we overemphasize things that happened recently (Recency bias), we don'prioritize time to prepare for it, and that we all suffer from many different biases when analyzing it. For example, contrasting people to us and using ourselves as the scale of excellence.

According to \Citet{luu2015normdeviance}, a dysfunctional Performance Review would disproportionally reward heroism and new features while overlooking people doing critical maitenance and bug fixing, especially for Promotion.

\Citet{youtube:Evrg8kSnVuM} argues that the current impact isn't a function of people who were working recently in the team, or actions did in-period, but the work done way before it. Current quarter results were fully baked a couple of years ago. You can either recognize work from others in the impact report of someone, or not recognize how their efforts will impact in the future.

Jeff Bezos on Long-term thinking and Impact:

“When somebody… congratulates Amazon on a good quarter… I say thank you. But what I’m thinking to myself is… those quarterly results were actually pretty much fully baked about 3 years ago. Today I’m working on a quarter that is going to happen in 2020. Not next quarter. Next quarter for all practical purposes is done already and it has probably been done for a couple of years.”

“It’s not natural for humans. So it’s a discipline you have to build. The get rich slowly schemes are not big sellers on infomercials. That’s something that you have to… teach over time,”

  • Jeff Bezos

2. Graph-based Performance Review

I idealized a Graph-based Performance Review (GPR). A system that we represent projects, people, documents, feedback, and impact statements as a graph, offering a convenient set of tools to insert and retrieve information from it. It generates performance reports automatically and cumulatively. It enables peer review of more granular activity, and makes it convenient to assess one's performance without depending on a person's manager.

Figure 1: A graph shows the relations between people, projects, and documents.

As everything these days, it has many GenAI features in potential, like automatically generating performance reports, letting people querying about a project, document, or person, and much more.

Figure 2: Asking questions in a graph item and let the AI agent navigate it to answer.

I did a prototype to make it easier to visualize it, and I will go through the main characteristics in the following sections - though it doesn't work exactly as intended and some are not well represented in the prototype since I hit my patience budget for vibe coding.

3. Information Ingestion

For such system to work, the information should flow to it as a natural step of accomplishing work. Thus, integrations with systems like Jira, Github, Google Drive, etc, are a must have.

For example, either documents following a certain pattern (a tag, an identifier in the title) should go automatically to it, or pasting its link should make it be integrated with ease with auto completing for its description and linked documents.

One can imagine the project granularity as a Jira epic, or being able to easily attach assets to a project elements in the form of Jira epics or GIthub repositories, which can automatically create the connections between internal projects referenced in the code.

Figure 3: Projects should have a very rich representation to make their consequences clear.

The person representation also should automatically be fed by other systems that keep their information updated: team, career level, position changes, contributions, feedbacks, etc.

Figure 4: Many filters could be applied in a person's view, but their profile should be cumulative to enable a more accurate picture of their contribution to the long-term success of a company.

4. Tools for ingesting meaningful information

A big issue with feedback is that they are hard to write, so most feedback is unhelpful: they lack context, facts, effect, or what they expected instead. AI assistants can help on extracting the most of feedbacks. The same goes for "Impact statements". We can often challenge claims, request facts, evidence, and help cover the most obvious challenges people would do when assessing that claim.

Figure 5: Pushing people to write better feedback using AI.

5. Make it easy to identify someone's contribution to an initiative

Even if someone creates a great impact statement for their performance report, it might lack how their involvement was important to it. Detailing it is hard because a project contains multiple people, and describing what they did is what useful to understand the scope of a certain individual.

For example, two Data Scientists worked in a project that generated a $10M savings. Both of them will report this number in their performance review. But how should we attribute their contribution to it?

To solve it, we need to know what the person did, and how their peers see their contribution.

Figure 6: A better naming and accessibility can help the peer review feature to provide the right weight to people's contributions

It is likely that we can automatically aggregate this information to make each person's involvement clear in a timeline of a project.

6. Maintenance work is easier to visualiza

Maintaining an important project means associating yourself to a node that has many connections. One does not need major release in the period to make their work visible. Their activity to maintain an important node will be captured by the system.

Figure 7: The ability to connect one project to another helps with the proper attribution of projects that are foundational and usually get lower visibility. Or projects that take a while until they support important initiatives and show their real value.

It can be extremely useful to internal platforms, too. Because we can link internal tools to the projects they support, showing more pratically the importance of that tool in the day to day as different people navigate the graph.

7. The test of the time

It is common to see rewards for leading indicators that fail short. A good system for performance evaluation should make it easy to evaluate how previous contributions evolved. It will make it easier to recognize things that take longer to deliver value, while it can also hold cases where people over sell projects in initial stages, but are unable to carry it through.

Moreover, a performance report is generated including statements about influence and impact of documents and projects even though the person's activity that generated them happened in a previous period.

8. Investigating high-leverage cases

Promotions, especially for very senior levels, are extremely important to get right. In general, promotions are the only public reward signal a company shares about doing things right. When someone is promoted, they shape others behaviors. Thus, wrong promotions are a catastrophe.

However, preparing promo packs is also very time-consuming, and one might not be able to make everything needed by the promo committee, or whatever process a company uses for promotion.

We can facilitate investigation using the AI Agent and let people ask questions about a person's work.

Figure 8: One can do deep dives about someone's performance and achievements for high-leverage decisions like a promotion, without generating questions to their manager.

<IMG>

Filters can also help to see people doing the same kind of work in the proposed new level of an employee, making the next scope more tangible and comparable.

Figure 9: Filter people by their expertise, career level, team, and find good examples of operating in different scopes.

Finally, the system itself could challenge or support a promotion nomination based on information it could extract from the graph. The AI Agent could have access to the career ladder, peer feedback, contributions, etc, and preapre it.

9. Automated performance reports

After a couple of years managing people, I created an algorithm to write their performance review that I would execute manually. In general lines, I would place together all the input from different sources (activities, impact statements, peer feedback, self-assessment, my feedbacks and assessment), and find the patterns, collect the evidence, and go after some clarification.

I think all of it can be automated and completely ease a people manager from it. A performance report generator should be available at all times, so people can also summarize their periods for checking their opportunities continuously.

Figure 10: Generate reports by choosing a period. Automatically compiles, mixes, and contrast all sources. It could also challenge what is unclear and suggesting the person or the manager going after specific information.

10. Peer review everything

To ensure things are grounded on reality, people can upvote or challenge many different items, like the contributions, the association of a project with a document/project, or the performance statements. We could either make everything public or think of an accessibility policy.

This should appear in the performance review report to provide the confidence on that information, or at least to make people go investigate and demand clarification.

Figure 11: Peers and other people must have a say on others contributions to ensure they are grounded on value generated to the business and to others working together. In the image, a contribution is highlighted in red because it had a challenge rate higher than 50% - a very arbitrary way of doing it.

11. Automated feedback & many other career supporting AI assistance features

If one's work is well represented in the graph, and an AI Agent has access to it, why couldn't it feedback the person using it? It can contrast the expectations about that person work with their contributions and deliver feedback as soon as it can see a pattern.

It can also suggest people one can talk to after it identifies someone is contributing to a particular type of project. Or projects, materials, and other resources one should look into to succeed on it.
