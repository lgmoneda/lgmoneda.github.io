David Barber

It's asked about the probability of a person being a terrorist considering he was identified as one by the scan. That is $P(B=T \mid S=T)$. To calculate it, the Bayes' Theorem can be used: $$ P(A \mid B) = \frac{P(B \mid A) \, P(A)}{P(B)} $$ $$P(B=T \mid sS=T) = \frac{P(S=T \mid B=T) * P(B=T)}{P(S=T)}$$ The components are known except for $P(S=T)$. Using the law of total probability, it can be found: $$P(S=T) = \sum_{T, C} P(S=T \mid B=x)*P(B=x) $$ $$ P(S=T) = P(S=T \mid B=T) * P(B=T) + P(S=T \mid B=C) * P(B=C) $$ $$P(S=T) = 0.95*0.001 + 0.05 * 0.99 $$ $$P(S=T) = 0.059 $$ Then: $$P(B=T \mid S=T) = \frac{0.95 * 0.01}{0.059}$$ $$P(B=T \mid S=T) = 0.161$$ So the probability that the identified terrorist is indeed a terrorist is 0.161.
